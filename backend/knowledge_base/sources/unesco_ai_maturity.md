> Focus: Public and private sector AI readiness across 6 pillars

## Overview

The UNESCO framework is designed primarily for assessing organizational and national AI readiness. It provides a holistic view across six interconnected pillars relevant to both public administrations and private enterprises.

## Six Pillars

### 1. Strategy & Vision
- National/organizational AI strategy
- Policy coherence
- International cooperation
- Long-term vision

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] AI strategy document exists with clear vision and goals
- [ ] Strategy reviewed and updated regularly (at least annually)
- [ ] AI strategy aligns with broader digital transformation goals
- [ ] International standards and frameworks referenced
- [ ] Stakeholder consultation in strategy development

### 2. People & Skills
- AI literacy across the organization
- Specialized talent pipeline
- Education and training programs
- Diversity in AI teams

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] AI literacy training available for all employees
- [ ] Specialized AI/ML training for technical teams
- [ ] Recruitment strategy targets AI talent gaps
- [ ] Diversity and inclusion goals for AI teams defined
- [ ] Partnerships with universities/research institutions

### 3. Technology & Infrastructure
- Computing resources
- Open-source adoption
- Interoperability standards
- R&D capabilities

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] Sufficient computing infrastructure for AI workloads
- [ ] Open-source tools and frameworks actively used
- [ ] Interoperability standards defined for AI systems
- [ ] Sandbox/experimentation environments available
- [ ] Technology refresh cycle includes AI capabilities

### 4. Operations & Processes
- AI project management
- Agile development practices
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Measurement and evaluation

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] Standardized AI project management methodology
- [ ] Cross-functional teams for AI initiatives
- [ ] Regular progress reviews and retrospectives
- [ ] Change management process for AI-driven changes
- [ ] Operational metrics for AI projects tracked

### 5. Governance & Regulation
- Regulatory compliance
- Ethical guidelines
- Accountability mechanisms
- Public trust building

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] AI governance framework documented
- [ ] Ethical guidelines for AI development and deployment
- [ ] Accountability mechanisms for AI decisions
- [ ] Transparency practices (public AI registers, disclosures)
- [ ] Compliance with relevant regulations (EU AI Act, GDPR, etc.)

### 6. Data Ecosystem
- Data availability and quality
- Open data initiatives
- Data sharing frameworks
- Privacy protection

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] Data quality management program in place
- [ ] Data sharing agreements and standards established
- [ ] Open data contributions where appropriate
- [ ] Privacy impact assessments for AI data usage
- [ ] Data lineage tracking implemented

## UNESCO Ethical AI Values

1. **Proportionality** — AI use proportionate to the objective
2. **Safety & Security** — Preventing harm throughout the lifecycle
3. **Fairness & Non-discrimination** — Equitable access and outcomes
4. **Sustainability** — Environmental and social sustainability
5. **Privacy** — Protection of personal data
6. **Human Oversight** — Human control and intervention capability
7. **Transparency & Explainability** — Understandable AI systems
8. **Responsibility & Accountability** — Clear attribution of AI outcomes
9. **Awareness & Literacy** — Public understanding of AI
10. **Multi-stakeholder Governance** — Inclusive decision-making
