## Overview

Google's AI Adoption Framework helps organizations understand where they are on their AI journey and what they need to do to advance. It evaluates maturity across **four pillars** and **six themes**, defining **three maturity phases**.

## Four Pillars

### 1. People
- AI talent acquisition and development
- Cross-functional AI literacy
- Executive sponsorship and champion network
- Center of Excellence (CoE) formation

### 2. Process
- AI project lifecycle management
- Agile/iterative development practices
- Change management for AI adoption
- Cross-team collaboration frameworks

### 3. Technology
- Cloud infrastructure readiness
- MLOps pipeline maturity
- API-first architecture
- Scalable compute and storage

### 4. Data
- Data quality and accessibility
- Unified data platforms
- Data governance and ownership
- Feature stores and data pipelines

## Six Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Learn** | Building AI knowledge across the organization |
| **Lead** | Executive sponsorship and strategic vision |
| **Access** | Data accessibility and infrastructure readiness |
| **Scale** | Moving from pilots to enterprise-wide deployment |
| **Automate** | Operational efficiency through AI automation |
| **Secure** | Security, privacy, and compliance integration |

## Three Maturity Phases

### Phase 1: Tactical
- AI used for isolated, departmental problems
- Limited data infrastructure
- Few AI-skilled employees
- No formal AI governance

#### Indicators:
- [ ] AI initiatives are ad-hoc, driven by individual teams
- [ ] Data exists in silos with limited cross-team accessibility
- [ ] AI skills concentrated in a small team or external consultants
- [ ] No formal AI strategy document exists
- [ ] Limited or no MLOps practices

### Phase 2: Strategic
- AI integrated into business strategy
- Centralized data platforms emerging
- Dedicated AI teams and CoE
- Formal governance and ethics frameworks

#### Indicators:
- [ ] AI projects aligned with business KPIs
- [ ] Data platform consolidation underway
- [ ] Center of Excellence established
- [ ] MLOps pipelines for model deployment
- [ ] AI governance policies documented
- [ ] Regular model performance monitoring
- [ ] Cross-functional AI project teams

### Phase 3: Transformational
- AI fundamentally reshapes business model
- Data-driven culture pervasive
- AI embedded in all major processes
- Continuous innovation and experimentation

#### Indicators:
- [ ] AI drives new revenue streams or business models
- [ ] Real-time data processing at scale
- [ ] Autonomous decision-making systems in production
- [ ] Organization-wide AI literacy achieved
- [ ] Proactive AI ethics and responsible AI practices
- [ ] Continuous experimentation culture (A/B testing, rapid iteration)
- [ ] AI agents integrated into daily workflows

## Checklist Items

### Strategy & Leadership
- [ ] Formal AI strategy aligned with business objectives exists
- [ ] Executive sponsor identified and actively engaged
- [ ] AI investment roadmap with measurable milestones defined
- [ ] Success metrics (ROI, efficiency gains) tracked regularly

### Data Readiness
- [ ] Data inventory across the organization cataloged
- [ ] Data quality standards established and monitored
- [ ] Cross-functional data sharing mechanisms in place
- [ ] Data governance framework (ownership, access, retention) active

### Technology Stack
- [ ] Cloud infrastructure capable of supporting AI workloads
- [ ] MLOps pipeline (training, deployment, monitoring) operational
- [ ] API gateways for AI service integration
- [ ] Feature stores or shared data processing pipelines

### Talent & Culture
- [ ] AI training programs for non-technical staff
- [ ] Dedicated AI/ML engineering team
- [ ] Cross-functional collaboration on AI projects
- [ ] Innovation time/hackathons for AI experimentation
