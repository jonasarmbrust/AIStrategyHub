# EU AI Act — Compliance Framework

> Source: European Union Regulation (EU) 2024/1689
> URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689
> Type: Mandatory regulation (EU member states)
> Enforcement: Phased from Feb 2025 to Aug 2027

## Overview

The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive, risk-based AI regulation. It classifies AI systems into four risk tiers and imposes obligations proportional to the risk level.

## Implementation Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Feb 2, 2025 | Prohibited AI practices banned; AI literacy obligations begin |
| Aug 2, 2025 | GPAI model rules; governance structures active |
| Aug 2, 2026 | **Primary enforcement** — High-risk AI system rules (Annex III) |
| Aug 2, 2027 | High-risk AI in EU-regulated products |

## Risk Classification

### Tier 1: Unacceptable Risk (PROHIBITED)
- Social scoring by governments
- Real-time biometric surveillance (with exceptions)
- Manipulation of vulnerable groups
- Emotion recognition in workplace/education
- Untargeted scraping of facial images

### Tier 2: High-Risk
**Systems requiring full compliance regime:**
- Recruitment and HR management
- Credit scoring and financial services
- Critical infrastructure management
- Education and vocational training
- Law enforcement and judicial systems
- Migration and border control
- Democratic processes

### Tier 3: Limited Risk (Transparency Requirements)
- Chatbots and conversational AI
- AI-generated content (deepfakes)
- Emotion recognition systems
- Biometric categorization

### Tier 4: Minimal Risk
- Spam filters, AI in video games
- No specific obligations, best practices encouraged

## Compliance Checklist

### Discovery & Classification
- [ ] Complete inventory of all AI systems (developed, procured, deployed)
- [ ] Role identification (Provider, Deployer, Importer, Distributor)
- [ ] Risk categorization applied to each AI system
- [ ] Prohibited AI practices reviewed and discontinued
- [ ] GPAI model obligations assessed

### Governance & Risk Management (High-Risk)
- [ ] Risk management system established (continuous, lifecycle-wide)
- [ ] Data governance controls implemented (quality, bias, representativeness)
- [ ] Technical documentation maintained and available for regulators
- [ ] Data sources and collection methods documented

### Operational Requirements
- [ ] Human oversight mechanisms designed and tested
- [ ] Users informed when interacting with AI systems
- [ ] Synthetic/AI-generated content properly labeled
- [ ] Automated logging and audit trails implemented
- [ ] Incident response process with authority reporting (72h for critical)

### Accountability
- [ ] Conformity assessment performed (internal or Notified Body)
- [ ] EU Declaration of Conformity documented
- [ ] CE marking applied (where applicable)
- [ ] AI literacy training programs implemented
- [ ] Third-party vendor contracts reviewed for compliance support

## Key Definitions

- **AI System**: Machine-based system that infers outputs (predictions, decisions, recommendations, content) from inputs
- **Provider**: Entity that develops or commissions an AI system and places it on the market
- **Deployer**: Entity that uses an AI system under its authority (except personal use)
- **High-Risk**: AI systems listed in Annex III or embedded in regulated products
