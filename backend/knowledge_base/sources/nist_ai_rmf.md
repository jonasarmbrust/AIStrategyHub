# NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

> Source: National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
> URL: https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Playbook
> Type: Voluntary, non-prescriptive framework
> Last Updated: 2023 (with ongoing Playbook updates)

## Overview

The NIST AI RMF is designed to help organizations manage risks associated with AI systems throughout their lifecycle. It is structured around **four core functions**: Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage.

## Core Functions

### 1. GOVERN — Cultivate Risk Management Culture

**Purpose**: Establish organizational policies, roles, and accountability structures for AI risk management.

#### Key Subcategories:
- **GOV-1**: Policies reflecting risk management are defined and implemented
- **GOV-2**: Accountability structures for AI risk management are in place
- **GOV-3**: Workforce diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility (DEIA) processes are prioritized
- **GOV-4**: Organizational teams are committed to a culture of responsible AI
- **GOV-5**: Processes are in place for robust engagement with relevant AI actors
- **GOV-6**: Policies and procedures are in place to address AI risks and benefits from third-party entities

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] Organization has documented AI risk management policies
- [ ] Clear roles and responsibilities for AI oversight are defined
- [ ] AI governance board or committee exists
- [ ] Third-party AI vendor assessment process is established
- [ ] Regular AI risk reviews are scheduled and conducted
- [ ] DEIA considerations are integrated into AI development lifecycle

### 2. MAP — Context and Risk Identification

**Purpose**: Identify and understand AI system contexts, intended purposes, and associated risks.

#### Key Subcategories:
- **MAP-1**: Intended purposes, context of use, and assumptions are established
- **MAP-2**: Categorization of the AI system is performed
- **MAP-3**: AI system benefits and costs are assessed
- **MAP-4**: Risks and impacts related to the specific context are identified
- **MAP-5**: Impacts to individuals, groups, communities are characterized

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] AI use cases are formally documented with intended purposes
- [ ] Risk categorization (high/medium/low) applied to each AI system
- [ ] Stakeholder impact assessments conducted
- [ ] Data provenance and quality requirements defined
- [ ] Potential negative impacts (bias, fairness) documented
- [ ] Cost-benefit analyses for AI initiatives exist

### 3. MEASURE — Assessment and Monitoring

**Purpose**: Employ quantitative and qualitative methods to analyze, assess, and monitor AI risks.

#### Key Subcategories:
- **MEA-1**: Appropriate methods and metrics identified
- **MEA-2**: AI systems evaluated for trustworthy characteristics
- **MEA-3**: Mechanisms for tracking identified AI risks over time
- **MEA-4**: Measurement of feedback about efficacy of risk controls

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] KPIs and metrics for AI system performance are defined
- [ ] Regular model evaluation and testing cycles established
- [ ] Bias and fairness metrics are tracked continuously
- [ ] Model drift monitoring is implemented
- [ ] A/B testing or canary deployment practices exist
- [ ] Audit trails and logging for AI decisions are maintained

### 4. MANAGE — Risk Mitigation and Response

**Purpose**: Manage identified risks through prioritization, mitigation, and response strategies.

#### Key Subcategories:
- **MAN-1**: AI risks based on assessments are prioritized and acted upon
- **MAN-2**: Strategies to maximize AI benefits and minimize negative impacts
- **MAN-3**: AI risks and benefits from third-party resources are managed
- **MAN-4**: Risk treatments include plans for deployment, operation, and monitoring

#### Checklist Items:
- [ ] Risk mitigation plans exist for identified AI risks
- [ ] Incident response procedures for AI system failures defined
- [ ] Rollback/shutdown mechanisms are available for AI systems
- [ ] Regular risk reviews lead to actionable improvements
- [ ] Lessons learned from AI incidents are documented
- [ ] Communication plans for AI-related incidents exist

## Trustworthy AI Characteristics (NIST)

1. **Valid and Reliable** — Accurate, consistent, and dependable performance
2. **Safe** — Does not endanger human life, health, property, or environment
3. **Secure and Resilient** — Resistant to attacks and recoverable from failures
4. **Accountable and Transparent** — Decisions can be explained and traced
5. **Explainable and Interpretable** — Outputs are understandable to stakeholders
6. **Privacy-Enhanced** — Protects personal and sensitive data
7. **Fair with Managed Bias** — Equitable treatment across demographics
